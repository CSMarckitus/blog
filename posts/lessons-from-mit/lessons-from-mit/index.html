Insights from an MIT Algebra I Lesson on Math and CS

October 2, 2024   ·   2 min read

People don’t understand, rather choose to memorize

I started watching the Linear Algebra lecture on MIT to refresh some concepts I had forgotten about math. After a few minutes, I started to realize that until that moment, I have not been able to really understand deeply the core foundations of Algebra.

It might seem like a simple or even silly perspective, but whenever I learn about matrices, a flood of thoughts and questions come to mind. So, let me share the one simple idea that really blew my mind and make my questioning more.

Do you know how to multiply matrices?

If you’ve ever studied higher-level math—at least in Spain—you probably remember matrix multiplication as a set of clunky, rigid steps that you just have to memorize and repeat carefully to avoid mistakes. For many, it feels more like a mechanical process than something intuitive.

In its simplest form, the only matrix multiplication we’re usually taught is:

$$ \begin{array}{c} \begin{bmatrix} a_{11} & a_{12} \\ a_{12} & a_{22} \end{bmatrix} \\ \text{A} \end{array} \times \begin{array}{c} \begin{bmatrix} b_{11} & b_{12} \\ b_{21} & b_{22} \end{bmatrix} \\ \text{B} \end{array} = \begin{array}{c} \begin{bmatrix} c_{11} & c_{12} \\ c_{21} & c_{22} \end{bmatrix} \\ \text{C} \end{array} $$

Where each c in the resulting matrix is calculated following this formula / system:

$$ C_{ij} = \sum_{k=1}^{n} A_{ik} B_{kj} $$

I used the formula to explain the system because diving too deep into examples here could make the post unnecessarily long and confusing. In computer science it is said that when something is difficult to explain, it’s often a sign that there’s a simpler or more intuitive way to approach it (though not always).

So what’s the alternative or the intuitive way to multiply matrices?

The importance of knowing where things came (developing intuition)

I’ve always searching where things came from, what are the motivations behind the people who create something, why they need it…

I think that making that, we can learn anything for forever because we develop a strong intuition that helps us to solve any problems related in the future although at first glance it may seem that it has no answer. Making that takes time, energy and sometimes a headache but it always pays off in the end.

Matrices are a shorthand way of writing systems of equations, although we can do more than that. So imagine the following simple equation:

$$ 2x - y = 0 \\ \text{It’s the same as:} \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} $$
